My Builds

Modded Coleman CT200U

Parts:

  • Predator 212cc engine
  • Mikuni VM22 Carburetor (22mm)
  • Centrifugal Clutch
  • #40 Chain
  • Exhaust (left-side mounted, curved over to right-side exit)
  • Hydraulic Brake Lever
  • Headlight
This build features a performance-focused setup with a Mikuni 22mm carburetor for smoother throttle response and power delivery. The exhaust system is custom-mounted starting on the left and sweeping over to the right for a unique look and improved clearance. Hydraulic brakes ensure better stopping power for off-road use. I am also trying to convince my friend to get it DMV approved by adding a title to it. All he would need to do is install turn signals and get PENNDOT-approved tires. While I could push this build for even higher speeds, I value my life since these machines aren't street legal and are best kept to controlled environments.

Both the governor limiter and stock flywheel were removed and replaced with a billet aluminum flywheel, increasing torque and allowing for higher RPMs. With these Stage 2 modifications, the bike can reach realistic top speeds of approximately 50-55 mph depending on gearing and terrain.

Custom Quad

Parts:

  • Predator 301cc engine welded to frame
  • Mikuni VM26 Carburetor (26mm)
  • 3/4 inch Torque Converter
  • Center-mounted Exhaust
  • Shock Absorber
  • Hydraulic Brakes (Front & Rear)
  • Headlight
This custom-built quad features a Predator 301cc engine welded directly to the frame for maximum durability. It uses a Mikuni VM26 carburetor for enhanced power and throttle response, and a center-mounted exhaust for balanced performance. With hydraulic brakes on both the front and rear and a fully functional suspension, it's built for aggressive off-road use while maintaining reliability.

Similar to the Coleman, I removed the engine's governor limiter and upgraded to a billet flywheel for enhanced torque and top-end speed. With the Stage 2 build, this quad is capable of reaching around 55-60 mph while maintaining strong off-road acceleration. I also plan to add a speaker system to the quad for music during rides, and unlike the Coleman, this build has the potential to be DMV approved with the right modifications for road use.
